## 1. STATE-BY-STATE LLC FORMATION

### Filing Entity by State

**Most states file with the Secretary of State (SOS).** Exceptions:

| State | Filing Entity |
|-------|--------------|
| Alaska | Dept. of Commerce, Community & Economic Development |
| Arizona | Arizona Corporation Commission |
| Hawaii | Dept. of Commerce & Consumer Affairs (DCCA) |
| Utah | Dept. of Commerce, Division of Corporations |

All other states + DC: **Secretary of State** (or Department of State in NY, FL, PA)

### Formation Document Name

States are grouped by the name of the formation document:

**"Articles of Organization" (majority -- ~35 states):**
Alabama (note: some sources say Certificate of Formation), Alaska, Arizona, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Vermont, Virginia, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming, DC

**"Certificate of Formation" (~8 states):**
Alabama, Delaware, Maine, Mississippi, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Texas, Washington

**"Certificate of Organization" (~7 states):**
Connecticut, Idaho, Iowa, Massachusetts, Nebraska, Pennsylvania, Utah

> **Note:** Some states appear in multiple lists due to conflicting sources and recent name changes. Always verify with the state's current filing portal. The functional document is identical regardless of name.

### Annual Report / Ongoing Fee Requirements

**No Annual Report Required:**
- Arizona
- Missouri
- New Mexico
- Ohio
- South Carolina (if taxed as partnership/disregarded)

**Biennial Report (every 2 years):**
- Iowa ($30 online)
- New York ($9)
- DC ($300)

**Annual Report Fee Ranges:**

| Fee Tier | States |
|----------|--------|
| $0-$25 | Arizona (none), Missouri (none), New Mexico (none), Ohio (none), New York ($9 biennial) |
| $25-$50 | Michigan ($25), Indiana ($31 online/$50 mail), Iowa ($30 biennial), Kansas ($55) |
| $50-$100 | Most states fall in this range |
| $100-$200 | California ($800 franchise tax), Nevada ($150 annual list + $200 business license) |
| $200+ | DC ($300 biennial), Massachusetts ($500), Tennessee ($300 min) |

> **California Warning:** $800 annual franchise tax for all LLCs (first year exempt for new LLCs formed after 1/1/2024).

### Special Requirements

**Publication Requirement:**
- **New York** -- Must publish in 2 newspapers for 6 weeks ($300-$1,500+ depending on county), plus $50 Certificate of Publication
- **Arizona** -- Must publish within 60 days of filing (cost varies, ~$50-$300)
- **Nebraska** -- Must publish in a legal newspaper for 3 consecutive weeks

### Registered Agent Requirement

**ALL 50 states + DC require a registered agent.** No exceptions.

Requirements:
- Must have a physical street address in the state (no PO boxes)
- Must be available during normal business hours
- Can be an individual or a business entity authorized to do business in the state
- Terms vary: "Registered Agent" (most states), "Resident Agent" (MD, DE), "Statutory Agent" (OH, AZ)

## 2. APOSTILLE BY STATE

### Issuing Office

**All 50 states: Secretary of State** handles apostilles (with minor naming variations).

Exceptions/Notes:
- Federal documents (FBI background checks, IRS docs, federal court docs): **U.S. Department of State, Office of Authentications** in Washington, DC ($20/document)
- Some states require county clerk authentication BEFORE the state apostille (notably New York for county-level documents)

### Fee Groups

| Fee | States |
|-----|--------|
| $2 | Rhode Island |
| $3 | Arizona |
| $5 | Ohio, Oregon, West Virginia |
| $5-$8 | Massachusetts ($6), North Dakota, South Dakota |
| $10 | Alabama, Alaska, Arkansas, Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Hawaii, Idaho, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, North Carolina, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming, DC |
| $15 | California, Florida, Maryland |
| $20 | New York, Washington |
| $25 | Delaware, Illinois, Texas |

> **Note:** Fees change regularly. The $10 tier is the most common. Always verify with the specific state's SOS website before quoting.

### Processing Time Groups

| Speed | States |
|-------|--------|
| Same Day (walk-in) | California (Sacramento office), Texas (Austin office), Florida, New York |
| 1-3 business days | Ohio, Virginia, most states with walk-in service |
| 3-5 business days | Average mail-in processing for most states |
| 5-10 business days | Standard mail-in for busier states |
| 10-20 business days | Arizona (mail-in), some states during peak periods |

### Submission Methods

| Method | Availability |
|--------|-------------|
| Mail-in | All 50 states accept mail-in requests |
| In-person/Walk-in | Most state capital SOS offices; some have satellite offices |
| Online | Growing number of states now accept online requests (CA, TX, FL, OH, VA, and others) |

### Expedited Service

Most states offer expedited processing for an additional fee ($5-$25 extra). Some states like Arizona charge $25 for expedited walk-in service with a 6-document limit.

## 3. NOTARY SERVICES & RON

### Remote Online Notarization (RON) Status (2026)

**47 states + DC have permanent RON laws enacted.**

**States WITHOUT permanent RON (as of early 2026):**
- Georgia (legislation pending)
- Mississippi (legislation pending)
- California (limited -- allows remote notarization of certain documents only)

> **Key Point:** All 50 states ACCEPT documents notarized via RON in another state. A notary commissioned in Virginia (first RON state) can notarize for someone in any state.

### RON Platform States (Early Adopters / Most Active)

Virginia, Texas, Florida, Nevada, Ohio, Indiana, Minnesota, Montana, Tennessee, Arizona, Colorado, Idaho, Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Michigan, Nebraska, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Utah, Vermont, Washington, Wisconsin, Wyoming

### Notary Fee Caps by State (In-Person Traditional)

| Fee Cap Range | States (Examples) |
|---------------|-------------------|
| $2 per signature | Iowa, Nebraska |
| $5 per notarial act | Connecticut, Kansas, Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont |
| $6 per notarization | Texas |
| $10 per notarial act | Alabama, Colorado, Georgia, Idaho, Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Montana, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Virginia, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming |
| $15 per signature | California, New York |
| $25 per notarial act | Florida, Nevada |
| No set maximum | Some states do not set caps; notaries set their own reasonable fees |

### RON Fee Caps

| Fee Cap | States |
|---------|--------|
| $25 per notarization | Texas, Virginia, Florida |
| $30 per notarization | Nevada |
| No specific RON cap | Many states; general notary fee caps may still apply |

### Reciprocity / Multi-State Notarization

- RON-commissioned notaries can typically notarize for signers in ANY state (the notarization is governed by the notary's state law, not the signer's state)
- Some states have reciprocity agreements for traditional in-person notarization
- The SECURE Notarization Act (federal) has been introduced to create nationwide RON standards but has not yet passed as of early 2026

## 4. EIN (FEDERAL -- SAME FOR ALL STATES)

### Overview

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Form | IRS Form SS-4 (Rev. December 2025) |
| Fee | FREE -- no charge from the IRS |
| Website | https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online |

### Application Methods

| Method | Processing Time | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| **Online** | Immediate (EIN issued instantly) | Available Mon-Fri 7am-10pm ET. Applicant must have valid SSN/ITIN. U.S.-based applicants only. |
| **Fax** | 4-6 business days | Fax completed SS-4 to (855) 641-6935. Include fax number for return. |
| **Mail** | 4-6 weeks | Mail to: Internal Revenue Service, Attn: EIN Operation, Cincinnati, OH 45999 |
| **Phone** | Immediate (international only) | Call (267) 941-1099 (not toll-free). For applicants without SSN/ITIN. |

### For LLC Factory Intake

- **Domestic clients:** Use online application (instant)
- **Foreign/non-resident clients:** Must use fax or phone (no SSN/ITIN for online)
- **Required info:** Legal name of LLC, state of formation, responsible party SSN/ITIN, business address, type of entity, reason for applying

## 5. BOI REPORTING (FEDERAL)

### MAJOR UPDATE: March 2026 Status

**As of March 26, 2025, FinCEN removed BOI reporting requirements for ALL U.S. domestic companies.**

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Governing Agency | FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network) |
| Form | BOI Report via https://boiefiling.fincen.gov |
| Fee | FREE |

### Current Requirements (Post-March 2025 Rule Change)

- **U.S. domestic companies:** EXEMPT from BOI reporting. No filing required.
- **Foreign companies registered in the U.S.:** Still required to file.
  - Registered before March 26, 2025: Deadline was April 25, 2025
  - Registered on/after March 26, 2025: 30 calendar days after registration

### For LLC Factory Intake

- **Domestic LLC clients:** No BOI filing needed (inform clients of exemption)
- **Foreign entity clients registering in the U.S.:** Must file within 30 days of registration
- **Monitor for changes:** The BOI landscape has been highly volatile (multiple court injunctions, reversals). Stay current with FinCEN updates.

## 6. STATE TAX REGISTRATION

### States with No Individual Income Tax (9 States)

| State | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Alaska | No income tax, no statewide sales tax (local sales taxes exist) |
| Florida | No income tax |
| Nevada | No income tax |
| New Hampshire | No income tax on wages (interest/dividends tax repealed 2025) |
| South Dakota | No income tax |
| Tennessee | No income tax on wages (Hall tax on investment income repealed 2021) |
| Texas | No income tax |
| Washington | No income tax (but has capital gains tax on high earners) |
| Wyoming | No income tax |

### States with No Sales Tax (5 "NOMAD" States)

| State | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Alaska | No state sales tax, but local municipalities can levy 1-7% |
| Delaware | No sales tax (has gross receipts tax on sellers instead) |
| Montana | No state sales tax (some resort/tourism areas have local taxes) |
| New Hampshire | No sales tax |
| Oregon | No sales tax |

### Franchise Tax States

States that impose a franchise tax, annual tax, or privilege tax on LLCs/entities:

| State | Type | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| **California** | Franchise Tax | $800/year minimum (first year exempt for new LLCs post-2024) |
| **Delaware** | Franchise Tax | $300/year flat fee for LLCs; corps based on shares |
| **Texas** | Franchise (Margin) Tax | Based on revenue; $0 if revenue under $2.47M (2026); no-tax-due threshold |
| **Illinois** | Franchise Tax | Being phased down; first $10K exempt starting 2025 |
| **Tennessee** | Franchise Tax | $0.25 per $100 of net worth, minimum $100 |
| **Alabama** | Business Privilege Tax | Minimum $100 |
| **Arkansas** | Franchise Tax | Minimum $150 |
| **Georgia** | Net Worth Tax | Based on net worth apportioned to GA |
| **Louisiana** | Franchise Tax | Phased out effective 2026 |
| **Mississippi** | Capital Stock Tax | Being phased down |
| **Minnesota** | -- | No separate franchise tax for LLCs |
| **Missouri** | -- | No franchise tax |
| **Nevada** | Business License Fee | $200/year (functions like franchise tax) |
| **New Hampshire** | Business Enterprise Tax | 0.55% of enterprise value tax base |
| **New York** | Filing Fee | Annual fee based on NY-source gross income ($25-$4,500) |
| **North Carolina** | Franchise Tax | $200 minimum for corps; LLCs taxed as corps pay it |
| **Oklahoma** | Franchise Tax | $1.25 per $1,000 of capital |
| **Vermont** | Franchise Tax | Applies to banks/financial institutions primarily |
| **DC** | Franchise Tax | 8.25% on taxable income |

### Sales Tax Registration

**States requiring separate sales tax permit/registration** (for businesses selling taxable goods/services):

All states WITH sales tax require registration if you have nexus. The 45 states + DC with sales tax all have their own registration process. Key points:

- **Economic nexus:** Since South Dakota v. Wayfair (2018), most states require sales tax collection if you exceed ~$100K in sales or 200 transactions in the state
- **Registration portal:** Most states use their Dept. of Revenue or Dept. of Taxation website
- **No cost:** Most states do not charge for a sales tax permit (exceptions: some charge $10-$50)

## 7. BUSINESS LICENSES

### States Requiring State-Level General Business License

These states require ALL businesses to obtain a state-level general business license regardless of industry:

| State | License Name | Issuing Office | Approx. Fee |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Alabama** | Business Privilege License | Dept. of Revenue | Varies by gross receipts |
| **Alaska** | State Business License | Dept. of Commerce | $50/year |
| **Delaware** | Business License | Division of Revenue (OneStop) | $75 |
| **DC** | Basic Business License | DCRA | $70-$200 |
| **Hawaii** | General Excise Tax License | Dept. of Taxation | $20 |
| **Nevada** | State Business License | Secretary of State | $200/year |
| **Washington** | State Business License | Business Licensing Service (BLS) | $10-$90 |
| **West Virginia** | Business Registration Certificate | State Tax Dept. | $30 |

### States with NO State-Level General Business License

These states handle business licensing at the **local (city/county) level only**:

Arizona, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming

> **Note:** Even states without a general state business license may require state-level **industry-specific** licenses (contractors, healthcare, food service, real estate, etc.).
