## The Model: How Drop-Ship Arbitrage Works

### The Core Loop

```
1. FIND  --> Product on Platform A at $X
2. LIST  --> Same product on Platform B at $X + margin
3. SELL  --> Buyer purchases on Platform B
4. ORDER --> You purchase from Platform A, ship to buyer
5. PROFIT --> Pocket the difference minus fees
```

### Why It Works at Scale

Drop-ship arbitrage has thin margins per unit. A single $0.50 profit sale seems pointless. But **volume is the multiplier**:

| Daily Units | Profit/Unit | Daily Profit | Monthly Profit | Annual Profit |
|------------|------------|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| 50 | $0.50 | $25 | $750 | $9,000 |
| 100 | $0.50 | $50 | $1,500 | $18,000 |
| 250 | $0.50 | $125 | $3,750 | $45,000 |
| 500 | $0.75 | $375 | $11,250 | $135,000 |
| 1,000 | $1.00 | $1,000 | $30,000 | $360,000 |

At 1,000 units/day with $1.00 average profit, you're earning $30K/month. The key insight: **you don't need big margins, you need big volume and automation.**

### The Math (Realistic Unit Economics)

**Average product profile at scale:**
- Source price: $5.00 (wholesale/clearance)
- Sell price: $14.99
- Platform fee (11% blended): -$1.65
- Shipping cost: -$5.50 (USPS Ground Advantage, under 1 lb)
- Packaging: -$0.50
- **Gross profit per unit: $2.34**
- Returns adjustment (5% rate, total loss): -$0.37
- **Net profit per unit: $1.97**

At 500 units/day: **$985/day = $29,550/month**

> See `revenue_scenarios.md` for detailed breakdowns across price points and platforms.

### Compliance Note

Platform policies matter. Pure drop-shipping from retail sites (buying on Amazon, shipping to eBay buyer) is banned on both eBay and Amazon. The compliant model is:
- **Wholesale sourcing** with legitimate invoices
- **Retail arbitrage where you hold inventory** (buy, store, ship yourself)
- **Direct supplier relationships** (CJ Dropshipping, Spocket, SaleHoo)

> See `account_health_playbook.md` for full policy analysis per platform.

## Risk Management (Top 5)

### 1. Account Suspension (CRITICAL)
**Risk:** Platform bans your account for policy violations, freezing listings and held funds.
**Mitigation:**
- Source from wholesale suppliers with legitimate invoices (not retail arbitrage drop-ship)
- Hold inventory yourself when sourcing from retail (buy, store, ship)
- Keep defect rate below 2% on all platforms
- Diversify: never put more than 40% of revenue on a single platform
- Monitor Account Health Rating daily via RelistApp dashboard

### 2. Price Race / Margin Collapse (HIGH)
**Risk:** Competitors undercut your prices until margins hit zero.
**Mitigation:**
- Automated repricing that floors at minimum margin (15%)
- Product rotation: exit saturated products within 2 weeks
- Focus on trending/new products where early-mover advantage exists
- Bundle products for differentiation
- Move to wholesale/private label for defensible margins (Month 3+)

### 3. Cash Flow Crunch (HIGH)
**Risk:** Platform payment holds (21 days for new eBay sellers) while you need to fund new purchases.
**Mitigation:**
- Start with $2,000 working capital minimum
- Prioritize platforms with fast payouts: Mercari (3 days), FB Marketplace (1-5 days)
- eBay: push through 25+ sales fast to unlock 1-3 day payouts
- Never commit more than 50% of available cash to unshipped inventory
- Track payment hold timelines per platform religiously

### 4. Returns Eating Margins (HIGH)
**Risk:** 10-15% return rates on certain categories destroy profitability.
**Mitigation:**
- Avoid high-return categories (clothing with fit issues, fragile electronics)
- Target low-return categories: phone cases (3-5%), pet supplies (2-4%), cables (5-8%)
- Accurate listings with real photos reduce "not as described" returns
- Factor 5% return rate into all margin calculations
- Kill products exceeding 10% return rate immediately

### 5. ToS Violations / IP Issues (CRITICAL)
**Risk:** Listing trademarked or restricted products results in listing removal, account strikes, or legal action.
**Mitigation:**
- Check brand restrictions before listing any product
- Avoid gated brands: Apple, Nike, LEGO, Disney, Nintendo
- Use generic/unbranded products for lowest risk
- Monitor VeRO/IP takedown notices and remove flagged products instantly
- Build a restricted brands database in RelistApp

## RelistApp Development Priorities

These features are ordered by revenue impact. Build them in this sequence.

### Priority 1: Product Scout (Highest Revenue Impact)
**What it does:** Scans wholesale suppliers and deal sources to find products with profitable arbitrage margins automatically.
**Why first:** Without product discovery, everything else is manual browsing. This is the engine.
**Key capabilities:**
- BSR scanner (Amazon Best Sellers Rank monitoring)
- Clearance/deal tracker across retail sites
- Category sweeper with minimum ROI filter
- Trend alert system (200%+ BSR jump notifications)

### Priority 2: Bulk Cross-Lister
**What it does:** Creates listings on multiple platforms simultaneously from a single product entry.
**Why second:** Manually listing the same product on 6 platforms takes 30 minutes. This should take 30 seconds.
**Key capabilities:**
- Single product entry, multi-platform output
- Platform-specific title/description optimization
- Auto-calculate price per platform (factoring different fee structures)
- Photo resize/format per platform requirements
- Template system for category-specific listings

### Priority 3: Price Monitor
**What it does:** Continuously monitors competitor prices and source prices, auto-adjusting your listings.
**Why third:** Margins change hourly. A product profitable at 8 AM might be a loss by noon if competitors undercut or source prices rise.
**Key capabilities:**
- Source price monitoring (alert if cost increases above margin threshold)
- Competitor price tracking per listing
- Auto-reprice within configurable min/max bounds
- Floor price enforcement (never sell below minimum margin)
- Price history charts for decision-making

### Priority 4: Order Router
**What it does:** When a sale occurs, automatically identifies the best source to fulfill from and initiates the order.
**Why fourth:** At 100+ orders/day, manual order processing is a full-time job. This eliminates it.
**Key capabilities:**
- Multi-source comparison (cheapest available source for each order)
- Auto-order from integrated suppliers (CJ Dropshipping, Spocket)
- Shipping label generation via PirateShip API
- Tracking number sync back to selling platform
- Out-of-stock detection and auto-pause of affected listings

### Priority 5: Analytics Dashboard
**What it does:** Real-time P&L, product performance, platform comparison, and trend visualization.
**Why fifth:** You can operate without analytics initially (spreadsheet works for Week 1). But scaling past 200 sales/day without data visibility leads to blind spots.
**Key capabilities:**
- Real-time P&L per product, platform, and category
- Best/worst performer rankings
- Return rate tracking per product and category
- Cash flow forecasting with payment hold visibility
- Trend alerts based on sales velocity changes
