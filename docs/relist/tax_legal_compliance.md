## 1. Sales Tax

### The Marketplace Facilitator Reality (2026)

The good news: **most marketplace platforms now collect and remit sales tax on your behalf.** You don't need to calculate, collect, or remit sales tax on marketplace sales in most states.

| Platform | Collects Sales Tax? | States Covered | Your Responsibility |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| eBay | Yes (Marketplace Facilitator) | All states with sales tax (46 states + DC) | None for marketplace sales |
| Mercari | Yes (Marketplace Facilitator) | All states with sales tax | None for marketplace sales |
| Poshmark | Yes (Marketplace Facilitator) | All states with sales tax | None for marketplace sales |
| Facebook Marketplace | Yes (Marketplace Facilitator) | All states with sales tax | None for marketplace sales |
| Your own website (Shopify) | **NO** — you must collect and remit | Only states where you have nexus | You handle everything |

**Key point:** As long as you sell exclusively through major marketplaces, the platform handles sales tax collection and remittance. This is one of the biggest advantages of marketplace selling vs. your own website.

### Economic Nexus (When You DO Need to Care)

If you sell on your own website (Shopify, WooCommerce) or at craft fairs/in-person, you must collect sales tax in states where you have **nexus** (a tax obligation).

**Types of nexus:**

| Nexus Type | What Creates It | Example |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Physical nexus | Presence in the state (office, warehouse, employees, inventory) | You live in Texas = nexus in Texas |
| Economic nexus | Exceeding a state's sales threshold | $100K in sales OR 200 transactions in California |
| Click-through nexus | Affiliate or referral relationships in a state | Rare for arbitrage operators |
| Marketplace nexus | Selling on a marketplace in the state | Handled by marketplace facilitator laws |

**2026 Economic Nexus Thresholds (Key States):**

| State | Threshold | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| California | $500,000 in sales | Highest threshold — most small sellers exempt |
| Texas | $500,000 in sales | Same as CA |
| New York | $500,000 AND 100 transactions | Must meet BOTH conditions |
| Florida | $100,000 in sales | Standard threshold |
| Pennsylvania | $100,000 in sales | Standard threshold |
| Illinois | $100,000 OR 200 transactions | Low transaction threshold |
| Washington | $100,000 in sales | Standard threshold |
| Most other states | $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions | De facto standard since Wayfair (2018) |

**For marketplace-only sellers:** You likely don't need to register for sales tax permits or file sales tax returns because the marketplace handles it all. If you expand to your own website and exceed economic nexus thresholds, then you must register, collect, and remit.

### Drop-Ship Nexus Complications

Drop-shipping creates a quirk: your source platform (Amazon) is shipping FROM a warehouse that may be in a different state than you. Some states argue this creates nexus for you because goods are shipping from within their borders.

**Practical reality for 2026:** This is largely a non-issue for marketplace sellers because marketplace facilitator laws override it. The platform collects the tax regardless of where the item ships from.

**If selling on your own website:** Consult a CPA. The drop-ship nexus question is state-specific and evolving. Services like TaxJar and Avalara handle this automatically.

## 2. Income Tax

### The $600 Reporting Threshold (2026)

As of 2026, payment processors and marketplaces must issue a **1099-K** if you receive **$600 or more** in gross payments in a calendar year.

| Platform | Issues 1099-K? | Threshold | When You Receive It |
|----------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| eBay (via Adyen) | Yes | $600 gross | January of following year |
| Mercari | Yes | $600 gross | January of following year |
| Poshmark | Yes | $600 gross | January of following year |
| PayPal | Yes (if used) | $600 gross | January of following year |
| Venmo | Yes (if used for business) | $600 gross | January of following year |

**Critical: The 1099-K reports GROSS revenue, not profit.** If you sold $50,000 on eBay but your cost of goods was $35,000 and fees were $6,500, you still get a 1099-K for $50,000. You must properly deduct expenses to show your actual taxable income.

**What happens if you ignore it:** The IRS receives a copy of your 1099-K. If you don't report the income, the IRS's automated matching system will flag you for an audit notice (CP2000). They'll assume the entire $50,000 is profit and send you a tax bill. You then have to prove your deductions.

### Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes for the year, the IRS requires **quarterly estimated tax payments.** Failing to pay quarterly results in underpayment penalties.

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Q1 | January 1 - March 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | April 1 - May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | June 1 - August 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | September 1 - December 31 | January 15 (next year) |

**How to calculate estimated payments:**

```
Estimated quarterly tax = (Projected annual net profit x effective tax rate) / 4

Example at $30K/month gross:
- Annual gross revenue: $360,000
- Annual COGS + fees: $288,000 (80%)
- Annual net profit: $72,000
- Federal tax (22% bracket): $15,840
- Self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35%): $10,147
- State tax (varies, assume 5%): $3,600
- Total annual tax: ~$29,587
- Quarterly payment: ~$7,397

Set aside 25-30% of net profit for taxes.
```

**IRS Form 1040-ES** — use this to make quarterly payments. Pay online at IRS.gov/payments (free), or use EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System).

Everything you spend to run your arbitrage business is deductible against your revenue. Track these meticulously.

| Category | Examples | Documentation Needed |

### Self-Employment Tax

As a sole proprietor or single-member LLC, you pay **self-employment tax** (Social Security + Medicare) on your net business income.

| Component | Rate | Applies To |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Social Security | 12.4% | First $168,600 of net earnings (2026) |
| Medicare | 2.9% | All net earnings |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% | Net earnings above $200,000 (single) |
| **Total SE tax** | **15.3%** | On 92.35% of net profit |

**The 50% deduction:** You can deduct HALF of your self-employment tax on your personal return (line adjustment, not itemized). This reduces your adjusted gross income.

## 3. Business Structure

### Sole Proprietorship (Default)

If you sell online without forming a business entity, you're automatically a sole proprietorship.

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| No formation cost | No liability protection |
| No separate tax return (report on Schedule C) | Personal assets at risk if sued |
| Simplest to operate | Less credible to suppliers and partners |
| No annual state fees | Cannot bring in partners easily |

**When it works:** Revenue under $10,000/month, testing the business, first 1-3 months.

### LLC (Recommended Before $10K/month Revenue)

A Limited Liability Company separates your personal assets from your business liabilities. If a buyer sues you or a debt goes bad, your personal savings, home, and car are protected (in most cases).

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Liability protection (corporate veil) | Formation cost ($50-500 depending on state) |
| Professional credibility | Annual state fees ($50-800/year depending on state) |
| Can open business bank accounts/credit | Separate bookkeeping required |
| Flexible tax treatment (can elect S-Corp) | Slightly more paperwork |
| Required for wholesale accounts (Tier 2+) | Must maintain corporate formalities |

**Formation costs by state (2026):**

| State | Filing Fee | Annual Fee | Notes |
|-------|-----------|------------|-------|
| Wyoming | $100 | $60 | Popular for out-of-state formation, no state income tax |
| Delaware | $90 | $300 | Business-friendly laws, best for investors/fundraising |
| New Mexico | $50 | $0 | Cheapest option, no annual report |
| Texas | $300 | $0 (unless >$2.47M revenue) | No state income tax |
| California | $70 | $800 (minimum franchise tax) | Expensive annual fee, but required if you live in CA |
| Florida | $125 | $138.75 | No state income tax |
| New York | $200 + publication ($500-2,000) | $9/biennial | Publication requirement is expensive |

**Recommendation:** Form your LLC in the state where you live and operate. Out-of-state formation (Wyoming, Delaware) adds complexity — you'd still need to register as a foreign LLC in your home state and pay fees there too.

**How to form an LLC:**
1. Choose a name (check availability on your state's Secretary of State website)
2. File Articles of Organization ($50-500)
3. Get an EIN from the IRS (free, online at IRS.gov, takes 5 minutes)
4. Open a business bank account with your EIN
5. File for any required local business licenses
6. Optional: draft an Operating Agreement (single-member LLC template is fine)

**DIY vs. service:**
| Option | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| DIY (state website) | $50-500 (filing fee only) | Cheapest, straightforward in most states |
| LegalZoom | $79 + filing fee | Adds convenience, some unnecessary upsells |
| Incfile/ZenBusiness | $0 + filing fee | Free formation, pay for add-ons |
| Attorney | $500-1,500 | Best if complex situation (partners, IP, multi-state) |

### S-Corp Election (At $50K+ Net Profit)

Once your net profit exceeds approximately $50,000/year, an **S-Corp election** can save you significant self-employment tax.

**How it works:**
- You pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (e.g., $40,000)
- The remaining profit is a distribution (not subject to SE tax)
- You save 15.3% SE tax on the distribution portion

**Example at $100,000 net profit:**

| Structure | SE Tax Base | SE Tax (15.3%) | Savings |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Sole Prop / LLC | $92,350 | $14,130 | - |
| S-Corp (salary $50K) | $50,000 | $7,650 | **$6,480** |

**Costs of S-Corp:**
- Payroll service ($20-50/month)
- Separate S-Corp tax return (Form 1120-S, CPA cost $500-1,500)
- More complex bookkeeping

**Recommendation:** Don't elect S-Corp until net profit consistently exceeds $50K/year. Below that, the payroll and accounting costs eat the tax savings.

## 4. Record Keeping

### What You Must Keep

The IRS requires you to keep records that support your income, deductions, and credits. For an arbitrage business, this means:

| Record | What to Keep | How Long |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Every source purchase | Order confirmation, receipt, amount, date, item | 7 years |
| Every sale | Platform sale record, amount, date, buyer, fees | 7 years |
| Every fee | Platform fee breakdown, payment processing fees | 7 years |
| Every return | Return reason, refund amount, outcome | 7 years |
| Every expense | Receipt or invoice for all business expenses | 7 years |
| Bank statements | All business account statements | 7 years |
| Credit card statements | All business credit card statements | 7 years |
| 1099-K forms | From every platform and payment processor | Permanently |
| Tax returns | Filed returns with all schedules | Permanently |
| Mileage logs | Date, destination, purpose, miles (if deducting vehicle) | 7 years |
| Home office records | Square footage, total home size, exclusive use documentation | 7 years |

**Why 7 years:** The IRS generally has 3 years to audit, but 6 years if they suspect underreporting of 25% or more. Keeping 7 years covers you completely.

### Monthly P&L Requirement

At $30K+/month gross, you should produce a monthly Profit & Loss statement. This is your single most important financial document.

**RelistApp generates this automatically** from your tracked orders, but here's what it contains:

| Section | Line Items |
|---------|-----------|
| Revenue | Gross sales by platform, minus returns/refunds = net revenue |
| COGS | Source purchases, source shipping, source taxes |
| Gross profit | Net revenue - COGS |
| Operating expenses | Platform fees, software, supplies, internet, phone, office |
| Net profit | Gross profit - operating expenses |
| Tax reserve | 25-30% of net profit set aside |
| Owner draw | What you paid yourself |

## 5. Platform-Specific Tax Reporting

### eBay Tax Reporting (2026)

| Report | Where to Find | What It Shows |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| 1099-K | Seller Hub > Payments > Tax Documents | Gross payment volume for the year |
| Transaction report | Seller Hub > Payments > Reports | Every transaction with fees, refunds, adjustments |
| Fee invoice | Seller Hub > Payments > Reports | Monthly fee breakdown |

**Common eBay 1099-K confusion:** The 1099-K includes gross sales INCLUDING the sales tax that eBay collected and remitted on your behalf. This inflates the reported number. You need to subtract marketplace-collected sales tax when reconciling with your actual revenue.

**Reconciliation formula:**
```
1099-K reported amount
- Sales tax collected by eBay (on your behalf)
- Shipping charges collected (if you charge separately)
= Your actual gross revenue
- Returns/refunds
= Net revenue (this is what you report on Schedule C)
```

## 7. Insurance Considerations

### General Liability Insurance

At $30K+/month, consider general liability insurance:

| Coverage | What It Protects Against | Typical Cost |
|----------|-------------------------|-------------|
| General liability | Buyer injury from product, property damage | $300-600/year |
| Product liability | Defective product causes harm | $400-1,000/year |
| Cyber liability | Data breach, online fraud | $200-500/year |
| Umbrella policy | Additional coverage above other policies | $200-400/year |

**Recommendation:** Get general liability insurance once revenue exceeds $10K/month. Product liability is important if you sell electronics, beauty products, or anything ingestible. A BOP (Business Owner's Policy) bundles general liability + property for $500-800/year.

**Providers:**
- Hiscox (online-focused businesses)
- Next Insurance (fast online quotes)
- Hartford (established small business insurer)
- State Farm / Allstate (local agent)

## 8. Intellectual Property Risks

### The VeRO Problem

eBay's Verified Rights Owner Program (VeRO) allows brand owners to report listings that infringe their intellectual property. This is the biggest legal risk for arbitrage sellers.

| Risk Level | Brand Type | Examples | Advice |
|-----------|-----------|---------|--------|
| Very High | Luxury brands | Louis Vuitton, Gucci, Chanel | Never sell without authenticating |
| High | Athletic brands | Nike, Adidas, Under Armour | Legitimate to resell, but listing wording matters |
| High | Entertainment IP | Disney, Marvel, Star Wars | Avoid unless licensed |
| Medium | Electronics | Apple, Samsung, Bose | Legit to resell, but use caution with "new" claims |
| Medium | Beauty | MAC, Sephora exclusives | Authorized reseller concerns |
| Low | Generic/unbranded | No-name kitchen gadgets, phone cases | Minimal IP risk |

**The first sale doctrine:** In the US, you have the legal right to resell a product you legitimately purchased. However, eBay's VeRO system isn't a court — brands can file takedowns that are difficult to contest even when you're legally in the right.

**Practical approach:**
- Avoid the high-risk brands entirely in Tier 1
- Keep purchase receipts for everything (proves legitimate acquisition)
- Don't use brand logos in your listing photos (use your own photos)
- Don't claim to be an "authorized dealer" or "official seller"
- If you receive a VeRO takedown, don't relist — move to a different product

## 9. State-Specific Business Licenses

Most cities and counties require a business license to operate. Check your local requirements:

| License Type | Where to Get | Cost | Required? |
|-------------|-------------|------|-----------|
| City business license | City clerk / city website | $25-200/year | Usually yes |
| County business license | County clerk | $25-100/year | Depends on county |
| Seller's permit / resale certificate | State tax authority | Free-$50 | Yes, if collecting sales tax |
| Home occupation permit | City planning department | $25-100 | Required in some cities for home businesses |
| DBA (Doing Business As) | County clerk | $10-100 | If operating under a name different from your LLC |

**Resale certificate benefit:** A resale certificate allows you to purchase inventory without paying sales tax (you collect it from the end buyer instead). This is primarily useful in Tier 2/3 when buying from domestic wholesalers.

| Revenue Level | Tax Approach | Estimated CPA Cost |
|-------------- |-------------|-------------------|
| $0-$10K/year net | DIY with TurboTax/FreeTaxUSA | $0-100 |
| $10K-$50K/year net | CPA for annual return, DIY quarterly | $300-800/year |

**Finding a CPA who understands e-commerce:**
- Search "e-commerce CPA" or "online seller accountant"
- Ask in reseller communities (Facebook groups, r/Flipping)
- Look for CPAs who specifically mention eBay, Amazon, or marketplace sellers
- Interview 2-3 before choosing — ask about their experience with marketplace 1099-K reconciliation
