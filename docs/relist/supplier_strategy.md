# RelistApp Supplier Strategy

## Three-Tier Supplier Progression for Drop-Ship Arbitrage

> Last updated: April 2026 | RelistApp v1.0
> Progression from day-one marketplace sourcing to private label products

---

## Overview

Every successful arbitrage operator follows the same path: start by reselling retail products, graduate to wholesale, and eventually sell your own branded products. Each tier unlocks better margins, more control, and greater defensibility.

The mistake most operators make is jumping tiers too early. Tier 1 teaches you what sells. Tier 2 teaches you supply chain. Tier 3 rewards you for knowing both.

---

## Tier 1: Marketplace Sourcing (Day 1-30)

### What It Is

You buy products at retail (or clearance) from Amazon, Walmart, and other online retailers, then relist them on eBay, Mercari, and Poshmark at a markup. The source retailer ships directly to your buyer.

### Why Start Here

- Zero upfront inventory investment (buy only after you sell)
- Learn what sells before committing capital
- Understand platform rules, fees, and buyer expectations
- Build seller reputation and feedback

### Source Platforms

| Source | Best For | Typical Margin | Notes |
|--------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Amazon | Wide selection, fast shipping (Prime 1-2 day) | 15-30% | Best for established items with stable pricing |
| Walmart.com | Clearance deals, rollbacks | 20-40% | Shipping can be slower (3-5 days) |
| Target.com | Seasonal items, home goods | 15-25% | Good clearance section online |
| Costco.com | Bulk items, bundles | 20-35% | Non-member can buy online (5% surcharge) |
| Home Depot / Lowes | Tools, hardware, seasonal | 15-30% | Heavy items — watch shipping costs |
| Kohl's.com | Fashion, home, stacking coupons | 25-45% | Kohl's Cash + coupons create deep discounts |

### Finding Profitable Products

**Manual scouting process:**
1. Browse Amazon Movers & Shakers (what's trending up)
2. Check the product on eBay — search completed listings (filter: Sold)
3. Calculate: eBay sold price - Amazon price - eBay fees (13.25% + $0.30) - tax (7%) = margin
4. If margin > $1.50 and sell-through > 50%, list it

**RelistApp Product Scout automation:**
- Scans 500+ products daily from configured source platforms
- Cross-references with eBay sold data in real time
- Calculates true margin after all fees, tax, and estimated return cost
- Ranks opportunities by margin x velocity x return-risk
- Filters out restricted brands and categories automatically

### Clearance & Deal Stacking

The real margins in Tier 1 come from clearance and coupon stacking:

| Strategy | How It Works | Typical Margin Boost |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Amazon Warehouse Deals | "Used - Like New" at 20-40% off, sell as used/open box on eBay | +15-25% margin |
| Walmart Clearance (online) | Filter by clearance, sort by discount | +20-35% margin |
| Cashback apps (Rakuten, Ibotta) | 1-10% back on source purchases | +2-5% margin |
| Credit card cashback | 2-5% on all source purchases | +2-5% margin |
| Subscribe & Save (Amazon) | 5-15% discount on recurring items | +5-10% margin |
| Price errors | Rare but lucrative, buy fast before corrected | Variable, huge |
| Coupons (Kohl's, Target Circle) | Stack manufacturer + store coupons | +10-20% margin |

### Capital Required

| Level | Starting Capital | Daily Sourcing Budget | Expected Monthly Revenue |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Minimum viable | $200 | $30-50 | $300-$600 |
| Comfortable start | $500 | $50-100 | $600-$1,500 |
| Aggressive launch | $1,000 | $100-200 | $1,500-$3,000 |

### Lead Time

- Amazon Prime: 1-2 day delivery to buyer
- Walmart: 2-5 day delivery
- Others: 3-7 day delivery

**Critical:** Platforms expect you to ship within 1-3 business days. Since you're drop-shipping, the source platform's delivery speed IS your shipping speed. Prioritize sources with fast, reliable shipping.

### Risk Level: LOW-MEDIUM

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Source price increases after listing | Medium | Low (cancel listing or absorb small loss) | RelistApp price monitoring, 6-hour checks |
| Source item goes out of stock after sale | Low | Medium (must cancel sale, hurts account) | Check availability before listing, keep popular sources |
| Return from buyer, source return window closed | Low | Medium (stuck with item cost) | Track source return windows, 20% reserve |
| IP complaint from brand owner | Low | High (listing removed, possible account strike) | Avoid Nike, Disney, luxury brands, supplement brands |
| Account suspension for drop-shipping | Very Low | Very High | Never use Amazon packaging/invoices, remove Amazon inserts |

### Tier 1 Transition Triggers

Move to Tier 2 when:
- [x] You've identified 5-10 product categories that consistently sell
- [x] You're doing 10+ sales per day consistently
- [x] You have $2,000+ in working capital
- [x] Your eBay account is established (90+ days, Above Standard)
- [x] You understand your buyer demographics and preferences
- [x] You're hitting the ceiling on margin (source prices are your bottleneck)

## Tier 2: Direct Wholesale (Day 30-90)

### What It Is

You purchase products directly from manufacturers or authorized distributors at wholesale prices (40-60% below retail), hold a small inventory, and sell at near-retail prices on marketplaces. Some items can still be drop-shipped from the wholesaler.

### Why Upgrade

- Margins improve from 15-30% to 35-55%
- More reliable supply (no out-of-stock surprises)
- Access to products not available at retail
- Legitimate business relationships open doors to better terms
- Some wholesalers offer drop-shipping (they ship to your buyers)

### Wholesale Platforms & Sources

| Platform | Type | MOQ Range | Best For | Verification Required |
|----------|------|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Alibaba | Manufacturer direct (China) | 50-500 units | Generic products, accessories, gadgets | No (but due diligence critical) |
| Faire | Curated wholesale marketplace | 1-50 units | Boutique items, handmade, artisan | Yes (business verification) |
| Handshake (Shopify) | Wholesale marketplace | Varies | Trendy consumer goods | Yes (Shopify store required) |
| Wholesale Central | Directory of US wholesalers | Varies | Wide range, established brands | No |
| Tundra | Wholesale marketplace | Low, often no MOQ | Home, beauty, food | Yes (business account) |
| Dollar Days | Bulk discount retailer | 1 case (12-48 units) | Low-cost items, party supplies | No |
| Wholesale2B | Drop-ship wholesaler directory | No MOQ (drop-ship) | Drop-ship specific, no inventory | Membership fee |
| DHgate | China direct (smaller quantities) | 1-50 units | Phone cases, accessories, jewelry | No |

### Wholesale Pricing Tiers

| Quantity | Typical Discount Off Retail | Example (Item retails $15) |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| 1-9 units | 0% (retail) | $15.00 |
| 10-49 units | 20-30% off | $10.50-$12.00 |
| 50-99 units | 35-45% off | $8.25-$9.75 |
| 100-249 units | 45-55% off | $6.75-$8.25 |
| 250-499 units | 50-60% off | $6.00-$7.50 |
| 500+ units | 55-65% off | $5.25-$6.75 |

### Capital Required

| Approach | Starting Capital | Inventory Investment | Expected Monthly Revenue |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Small test orders (Faire/Tundra) | $500 | $200-500 per order | $1,500-$3,000 |
| Standard wholesale (50-100 MOQ) | $2,000 | $500-2,000 per order | $3,000-$8,000 |
| Aggressive wholesale | $5,000 | $2,000-5,000 per order | $8,000-$20,000 |

### Lead Time

| Source Location | Lead Time | Shipping Cost |
|----------------|-----------|---------------|
| US wholesaler | 3-7 business days | $5-20 (often free over $500) |
| Alibaba (standard) | 15-30 days (sea freight) | $3-8/kg sea, $8-15/kg air |
| Alibaba (express) | 7-15 days (air freight) | $8-15/kg |
| Faire/Tundra | 3-10 business days | Often free over order minimum |

### How to Evaluate a Wholesale Supplier

**Due diligence checklist:**

| Check | How | Red Flag |
|-------|-----|----------|
| Business legitimacy | Search state business registry, check BBB | No registration, no physical address |
| Product quality | Order samples before bulk ($20-50 investment) | Won't send samples, or samples are poor quality |
| Communication | Email response time, clarity, English proficiency | Slow responses, evasive answers |
| Payment terms | Wire transfer, PayPal, trade assurance | Wire only with no protection, crypto only |
| Return policy | Defective item handling, damaged in transit | No returns whatsoever |
| References | Ask for 2-3 existing customer references | Won't provide any |
| Minimum order | Reasonable for your stage | MOQ of 1,000+ units for unproven product |

### Inventory Management (New Challenge)

With wholesale, you're holding inventory for the first time. New risks:

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Dead stock (doesn't sell) | Test with small order first, never buy 500 units of something you haven't sold |
| Storage costs | Start with a spare room/garage, graduate to a storage unit ($50-150/mo) |
| Cash tied up in inventory | Follow the 30% rule — never put more than 30% of capital into a single product |
| Damage/loss | Keep inventory organized, climate-controlled if needed |
| Obsolescence | Avoid trending/seasonal items in bulk — stick to evergreen products |

**RelistApp inventory tracking:**
- Tracks units in stock per SKU
- Calculates days of supply remaining at current sell rate
- Alerts when inventory drops below reorder point
- Tracks landed cost per unit (purchase price + shipping + customs if applicable)
- Shows inventory value and aging

### Risk Level: MEDIUM

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Product doesn't sell at projected volume | Medium | Medium (stuck with inventory) | Test with Tier 1 first, small first orders |
| Supplier sends defective products | Low-Medium | Medium (returns, refunds, waste) | Samples first, start with reputable platforms (Faire) |
| Alibaba scam | Low (with Trade Assurance) | High (lost payment) | Always use Trade Assurance, never wire money directly |
| Shipping damage | Low | Low (file claim) | Require shipping insurance on orders over $200 |
| Import duties/customs (international) | Certain | Low (plan for it) | Factor 5-15% customs duty into landed cost |

### Tier 2 Transition Triggers

Move to Tier 3 when:
- [x] You've found 3-5 products that sell 50+ units/month consistently
- [x] You have established relationships with 2-3 reliable suppliers
- [x] Working capital exceeds $5,000
- [x] Monthly revenue consistently above $10,000
- [x] You understand your customer deeply (demographics, preferences, pain points)
- [x] You see an opportunity to differentiate (better packaging, bundles, branding)

## Tier 3: Private Label (Day 90+)

### What It Is

You create your own branded versions of products that are already proven sellers. You work with a manufacturer (usually via Alibaba) to produce items with your brand name, logo, and custom packaging. You sell them as your own brand on eBay, Amazon, Poshmark, and eventually your own Shopify store.

### Why Upgrade

- Margins of 50-70% (vs. 15-30% in Tier 1, 35-55% in Tier 2)
- Brand equity — you own something that has value beyond the next sale
- No competition on your exact listing (you're the only seller of your brand)
- Eligible for Amazon Brand Registry (A+ content, brand store, IP protection)
- Defensible business — no one can undercut you with the same branded product

### Private Label Process

**Step 1: Product Selection (1-2 weeks)**

Choose a product that:
- You've already proven sells well in Tier 1 or Tier 2
- Is simple to manufacture (no electronics, no complex mechanics)
- Has room for improvement (better packaging, included accessories, color options)
- Has healthy demand (1,000+ monthly searches on eBay/Amazon)
- Is not dominated by a major brand (avoid competing with Apple, Nike, etc.)

**Best private label categories for beginners:**
| Category | Examples | Typical Margin | Complexity |
|----------|---------|---------------|------------|
| Phone accessories | Cases, screen protectors, chargers | 60-75% | Low |
| Kitchen gadgets | Utensils, organizers, silicone mats | 55-70% | Low |
| Beauty/skincare | Brushes, tools, organizers | 60-75% | Low-Medium |
| Pet accessories | Collars, toys, bowls | 55-70% | Low |
| Home organization | Storage bins, drawer dividers | 50-65% | Low |
| Fitness accessories | Bands, yoga mats, water bottles | 55-70% | Low-Medium |

**Step 2: Find a Manufacturer (2-4 weeks)**

| Platform | Best For | How to Use |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Alibaba | Widest selection, competitive pricing | Search product, filter by "Verified" and "Trade Assurance", contact 5-10 suppliers |
| GlobalSources | Higher-quality manufacturers | Same process, tends to have more established factories |
| 1688.com | Lower prices than Alibaba (Chinese domestic) | Requires agent or Mandarin, but prices are 20-30% lower |

**Supplier evaluation for private label:**
- Request product samples from 3-5 manufacturers ($30-100 total)
- Compare quality, packaging options, customization flexibility
- Ask about MOQ for customized products (usually 100-500 units)
- Negotiate: first order at 50% of standard MOQ is common
- Confirm they can add your logo, brand name, custom packaging

**Step 3: Branding (1-2 weeks)**

| Element | Cost | Provider |
|---------|------|----------|
| Brand name | Free (brainstorm) | You |
| Logo design | $50-200 | Fiverr, 99designs |
| Packaging design | $100-300 | Fiverr, Canva (DIY) |
| Trademark filing | $250-350 | USPTO.gov (DIY) or attorney ($500-800) |
| UPC/EAN barcodes | $250 for 100 barcodes | GS1.org (official source, don't buy resold barcodes) |

**Step 4: First Order (2-6 weeks production + 2-4 weeks shipping)**

| Order Size | Investment | Risk Level | Recommendation |
|------------|-----------|------------|----------------|
| 100 units | $500-$1,000 | Low | Start here. Test the market. |
| 250 units | $800-$2,000 | Low-Medium | If you're confident from Tier 1/2 data |
| 500 units | $1,500-$4,000 | Medium | Only with proven demand data |
| 1,000 units | $2,500-$8,000 | Medium-High | Significant discount per unit |

**Step 5: Launch & Scale**

- List on all platforms with professional photos (hire a product photographer: $100-300)
- Use RelistApp to cross-list and manage inventory across platforms
- Run promoted listings on eBay (5-10% ad rate for launch)
- Gather reviews aggressively (eBay's review system, include insert cards asking for feedback)
- Reorder when you hit 30 days of supply remaining

### Capital Required

| Phase | Investment | Timeline |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Product research & samples | $100-300 | 2-4 weeks |
| Branding & design | $300-600 | 1-2 weeks |
| First production order (100 units) | $500-1,000 | 2-6 weeks production |
| Shipping (sea freight) | $200-500 | 3-4 weeks transit |
| Product photography | $100-300 | 1 week |
| **Total first product launch** | **$1,200-$2,700** | **8-16 weeks** |

### Margin Comparison Across Tiers

For a product that retails at $15.00 on eBay:

| Component | Tier 1 (Retail Arbitrage) | Tier 2 (Wholesale) | Tier 3 (Private Label) |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Source/COGS | $8.00 | $5.50 | $2.50 |
| eBay fees (13.25% + $0.30) | $2.29 | $2.29 | $2.29 |
| Sales tax on source | $0.56 | $0.39 | $0.00 (imported) |
| Shipping to buyer | $0.00 (free/baked in) | $3.50 (you ship) | $3.50 (you ship) |
| **Net profit** | **$4.15** | **$3.32** | **$6.71** |
| **Net margin** | **27.7%** | **22.1%** | **44.7%** |

**Note:** Tier 2 and 3 margins look different when you ship yourself due to shipping costs. However, Tier 3 COGS is so low that it more than compensates. Also, once you reach volume, negotiated shipping rates (UPS/FedEx contracts) drop shipping cost to $2.00-2.50.

### Risk Level: MEDIUM-HIGH

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Product doesn't sell (dead inventory) | Medium | High (capital locked up) | Only private-label products proven in Tier 1/2 |
| Quality issues from manufacturer | Low-Medium | High (returns, bad reviews) | Samples first, inspection service ($0.10-0.30/unit) |
| Customs/import issues | Low | Medium (delays, extra costs) | Use a customs broker for first shipment, learn the process |
| Competitor copies your product | Certain (eventually) | Medium | Trademark your brand, file DMCA/IP claims, keep innovating |
| Cash tied up for 2-3 months | Certain | Medium | Only invest capital you can afford to lock up |
| Amazon/eBay listing suspended | Low | High | Follow all platform rules, authentic product with proper UPC |

## Common Mistakes by Tier

### Tier 1 Mistakes
- Listing items from brands known for IP complaints (check eBay VeRO list)
- Not factoring in sales tax on the source purchase
- Forgetting to remove Amazon packaging indicators
- Over-investing before understanding payment hold timelines

### Tier 2 Mistakes
- Ordering 500 units of an untested product
- Not ordering samples first ("it looked good in the photos")
- Ignoring shipping costs in margin calculations
- Working with only one supplier (no backup)

### Tier 3 Mistakes
- Skipping the trademark (someone else trademarks your brand name)
- Choosing a product based on margin alone (ignoring competition and demand)
- Ordering too many units on the first run (start with 100, not 1,000)
- Poor packaging (the unboxing experience IS the brand)

---

*The three-tier progression is a staircase, not a ladder. Each step is stable and profitable on its own. You can stay at Tier 1 forever and make a living. But if you want to build a real business with real equity, the path leads through wholesale to private label. Let your data from each tier inform the next.*
