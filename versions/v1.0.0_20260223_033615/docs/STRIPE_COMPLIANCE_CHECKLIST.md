# Stripe Compliance Checklist

This document provides a comprehensive checklist for ensuring your EmpireBox website and business are ready for Stripe merchant account approval.

## Overview

Stripe requires specific business information, legal pages, and transparency measures before approving a merchant account. This checklist will help ensure you meet all requirements.

---

### Required Legal Pages
- [x] **Privacy Policy** - `/privacy`

## 🚀 Stripe Application Tips

### Before You Apply

1. **Complete Your Website**
   - Ensure all legal pages are published and accessible
   - Test all links in footer
   - Verify business address is displayed
   - Check that contact information works

2. **Business Consistency**
   - Business name should match across:
     - Legal documents
     - Website
     - Bank account
     - Stripe application
   - Use same email domain for all communications

3. **Clear Service Description**
   - Be specific about what you offer
   - Mention: "Multi-platform marketplace automation software"
   - Clarify it's a SaaS product
   - Note that it's digital goods/services

4. **Realistic Projections**
   - Don't inflate transaction estimates
   - Be honest about timeline (launching, pre-launch, etc.)
   - Provide reasonable monthly volume projections

### During Application

1. **Answer Questions Thoroughly**
   - Provide detailed, honest answers
   - Don't leave fields blank if optional
   - Explain your business model clearly

2. **Upload Clear Documents**
   - ID should be clear and legible
   - Bank statements should be recent (within 3 months)
   - Utility bills for address verification

3. **Business Bank Account**
   - Use a business bank account if possible
   - Personal account is okay for sole proprietors
   - Ensure account is in good standing

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **DON'T:**
- Leave legal pages incomplete or generic
- Use template text with [PLACEHOLDERS] visible
- Hide subscription terms in fine print
- Make auto-renewal unclear
- Omit refund policy
- Use a PO Box as business address
- Apply before website is ready
- Provide inconsistent business information
- Use personal PayPal/Venmo links alongside Stripe

✅ **DO:**
- Make all terms clear and prominent
- Use plain language in legal pages
- Display pricing transparently
- Clearly state auto-renewal
- Provide easy cancellation
- List physical business address
- Have functioning contact methods
- Keep business info consistent
- Only use Stripe for payments

## 🔍 Common Rejection Reasons

### 1. Incomplete Website
**Issue:** Legal pages missing, incomplete, or not accessible  
**Solution:** Complete all required pages and link them in footer

### 2. Unclear Business Model
**Issue:** Stripe doesn't understand what you're selling  
**Solution:** Provide clear service description, update homepage with clarity

### 3. High-Risk Indicators
**Issue:** Business seems like it could have high chargeback risk  
**Solution:** 
- Clear refund policy
- Good customer support information
- Transparent pricing
- Professional website

### 4. Address Issues
**Issue:** PO Box used, address doesn't match documents  
**Solution:** Use physical address, ensure consistency across all documents

### 5. Terms Not Clear
**Issue:** Subscription terms, auto-renewal, or refunds not clear enough  
**Solution:** Make these terms prominent, use bold text, add disclosures

### 6. Identity Verification Failed
**Issue:** ID documents unclear or don't match application  
**Solution:** Upload clear photos, ensure info matches exactly

## 📨 How to Respond to Stripe Requests

If Stripe asks for more information:

### 1. Respond Promptly
- Respond within 24-48 hours
- Don't wait until deadline

### 2. Provide Exactly What's Requested
- Read request carefully
- Provide specific documents asked for
- Don't send extra unrequested information

### 3. Be Professional
- Use proper grammar and spelling
- Be courteous and cooperative
- Provide clear explanations

### 4. Common Requests & Responses

**"Tell us more about your business"**
- Explain you're a SaaS company
- Detail the marketplace automation software
- Mention target customers (online sellers)
- Note it's subscription-based software

**"Update your legal pages"**
- Make specific requested changes
- Let them know when changes are live
- Provide direct URLs to updated pages

**"Verify your identity"**
- Upload clearer ID photos
- Provide additional verification documents
- Use well-lit, clear images

**"Clarify your refund policy"**
- Make refund terms more prominent
- Add specific timeframes
- Explain process clearly

## 📝 Timeline

Typical Stripe approval timeline:

- **Immediate**: If all information is perfect and business is low-risk
- **1-3 days**: If minor clarifications needed
- **1-2 weeks**: If identity verification or additional docs needed
- **2-4 weeks**: For complex businesses or international applications
