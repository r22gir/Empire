## Caption
A look at what's happening in the shop today.

The CNC is running a custom house number sign in walnut for a client in Bethesda, MD. This one's a clean modern font, recessed carving, finished with Danish oil to bring out the grain.

People ask us all the time what a CNC router actually does. Simply put — it's a computer-controlled cutting machine. We design the piece digitally, set up the toolpaths (the exact routes the cutting bit follows), load the material, and let the machine do the precision work.

The result is cuts and carvings that would be extremely difficult to achieve by hand — with repeatability and accuracy down to thousandths of an inch. It's not a replacement for hand craftsmanship. It's another tool in the shop that lets us build things we couldn't build otherwise.

After the CNC does its work, everything gets hand-sanded, finished, and inspected before it leaves the shop.

Want to see more of how things get made? Follow along — we'll be posting more process content from the shop. And if you want a custom sign, house numbers, or anything else CNC-cut, send us a message.
