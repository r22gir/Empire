## Caption
Why does custom drapery cost more than off-the-rack? Here's what goes into every panel we build.

1. Measuring — We measure your windows on-site. Every window is different, and even a half-inch matters for how a panel hangs and breaks at the floor.

2. Fabric selection — We source from professional-grade fabric houses. These aren't the same materials you'll find at a big box store. Better weight, better drape, better longevity.

3. Cutting and construction — Every panel is cut by hand, sewn with industrial machines, and built with proper header tape, weighted hems, and quality lining.

4. Pleat setting — Pinch pleats, goblet pleats, Euro pleats — each one is hand-set and individually sewn. This is what gives custom drapes their structure and shape.

5. Pressing and quality check — Before anything leaves the workroom, it gets pressed and inspected.

6. Installation — We hang and dress your drapes on-site so they fall correctly from day one.

That's the difference between custom and off-the-shelf. You're not just paying for fabric — you're paying for fit, construction quality, and a finished result that actually looks right in your home.
